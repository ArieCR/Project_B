OFDM vs. SC-FDMA

In LTE, the downlink (from base station to user) and uplink (from user to base station) use different technologies. The downlink relies on OFDM (Orthogonal Frequency-Division Multiplexing), while the uplink adopts SC-FDMA (Single-Carrier Frequency Division Multiple Access). The primary reason is that OFDM signals have a high Peak-to-Average Power Ratio (PAPR), which requires more expensive and less power-efficient amplifiers on mobile devices. SC-FDMA was introduced to solve this issue, offering lower PAPR while keeping many of OFDM's benefits.
