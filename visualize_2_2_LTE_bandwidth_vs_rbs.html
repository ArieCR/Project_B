How LTE Scales Bandwidth Without Touching Subcarrier Spacing

LTE locks the subcarrier spacing at 15 kHz. Instead of squeezing carriers closer together, the network adds more Resource Blocks (RBs) as channel bandwidth grows. The interactive grid shows how many RBs fit inside each standardized channel size.

2.2 LTE Standards

Every LTE Resource Block spans 12 subcarriers × 15 kHz, and it is the smallest unit the scheduler can hand out in the uplink or downlink. A wider channel simply means more RBs on the dial—from just six in a narrow 1.4 MHz carrier up to one hundred in the full 20 MHz allocation.
